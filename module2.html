Adult CPR

For adults, provide compressions at a rate of 100–120 per minute, at least 2 inches deep, allowing full chest recoil. Minimize interruptions and ensure proper hand placement in the center of the chest.

Child CPR

Use one or two hands for compressions depending on the size of the child. The depth should be about 2 inches (or one-third the depth of the chest). Rate remains 100–120 compressions per minute.

Infant CPR

Use two fingers for compressions just below the nipple line. Depth should be 1.5 inches (4 cm). Use the song “Stayin’ Alive” or “Baby Shark” to maintain correct rhythm.

Using an AED

Turn on the AED immediately and follow the prompts. Expose the chest and attach pads. Clear the victim before analyzing and delivering a shock if advised. Resume CPR immediately after.

Practice Reminder

Practicing CPR on dummies helps reinforce proper technique and rhythm. Repeated, hands-on training builds confidence and muscle memory.
