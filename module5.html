Why Team-Based BLS Matters

In a clinical or high-stakes setting, having multiple responders work together increases the effectiveness and efficiency of life-saving efforts. Clear roles and communication are key.

Common Roles in a BLS Team

  • Compressor: Delivers chest compressions.
  • Airway Manager: Provides rescue breaths or uses a bag-mask device.
  • AED Operator: Retrieves and applies the AED and operates it.
  • Team Leader: Oversees the situation, assigns roles, and ensures effective coordination.

Communication is Critical

Use clear, direct communication. Confirm instructions and announce actions (“Administering shock now!”). Rotate compressors every 2 minutes to avoid fatigue.

Use closed-loop communication: someone gives a direction, the receiver repeats it back, and confirms when it's done.

Coordinated Practice

Frequent team training helps improve timing, hand-offs, and decision-making. In high-performing teams, each person knows their role, when to rotate, and how to adapt if conditions change.
