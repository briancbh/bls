Why Team‑Based BLS Matters

In a clinical or high‑stakes setting, clear roles and communication are key to saving lives.

Common Roles in a BLS Team

  • Compressor – delivers chest compressions
  • Airway Manager – provides breaths or bag‑mask
  • AED Operator – retrieves/applies AED
  • Team Leader – coordinates & assigns roles

Communication is Critical

In a BLS emergency, clear communication prevents confusion and saves time.

Example:

  • Team Leader: “Jordan, start chest compressions.”
  • Jordan: “Starting compressions now.” (Jordan begins compressions)
  • Team Leader: “Avery, place the AED pads and prepare to analyze.”
  • Avery: “Placing pads now.” (Pads are applied)
  • Team Leader: “Is everyone clear? Deliver the shock.”
  • Avery: “Everyone clear. Delivering shock now.”

This closed-loop process confirms the task, repeats it for clarity, and confirms it's done.

Coordinated Practice

Regular team drills improve timing, hand‑offs, and decisions under stress. You should take time regularly to practice with your colleagues to make sure emergency situations go smoothly.
