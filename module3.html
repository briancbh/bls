What is an AED?

An Automated External Defibrillator (AED) is a portable device that checks the heart rhythm and can send an electric shock to restore a normal rhythm. AEDs are used to treat sudden cardiac arrest (SCA).

When to Use an AED

Use an AED as soon as it is available when a person is unresponsive and not breathing or only gasping. Start CPR immediately and continue until the AED is ready to use.

How to Use an AED

  • 1. Turn on the AED and follow voice prompts.
  • 2. Expose the person’s chest and attach the pads.
  • 3. Allow the AED to analyze the rhythm—do not touch the person.
  • 4. If shock is advised, ensure no one is touching the person and press the shock button.
  • 5. Resume CPR immediately after the shock, or if no shock is advised.

Pads Placement and Special Situations

Place one pad on the upper right chest and the other on the lower left side. If the person has a pacemaker or is wet, avoid placing pads over the device or water—dry the chest first.

Use pediatric pads if available for children under 8 years or 55 lbs (25 kg). If not available, adult pads can be used.
