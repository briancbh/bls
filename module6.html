Good Samaritan Laws

These laws protect individuals who offer reasonable help during emergencies. You are typically protected if:

  • You act in good faith
  • You provide care voluntarily
  • You avoid gross negligence

Post-CPR: Recovery Position

If the person regains consciousness and is breathing normally, place them on their side in the recovery position to keep their airway clear.

  • Roll the person onto their side gently
  • Bend their top leg and arm to stabilize the position
  • Continue to monitor their breathing until EMS arrives

First Aid Basics

In addition to CPR, you may encounter injuries requiring basic first aid. Here are quick actions:

  • Bleeding: Apply direct pressure with a clean cloth
  • Burns: Cool with water, cover with clean dressing
  • Fractures: Immobilize and wait for EMS

Bloodborne Pathogen Safety

Protect yourself when giving aid by treating all body fluids as potentially infectious:

  • Use gloves and a mask when available
  • Wash hands with soap and water immediately after providing care
  • Dispose of contaminated materials properly
